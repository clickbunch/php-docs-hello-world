Fashion Scandal!

Former Assistant at Chanel Exposes TRUE Price of Chanel, Hermes, Coach and Louis Vuitton Handbags...


Fashion brands are in a panic now that customers know their products are marked up as much as 20x the price they cost to make!

Vogue Magazine - A scandal is sweeping the fashion industry as a former assistant recently went public with a damaging admission:  Luxury brands like Chanel, Hermes, Coach and Louis Vuitton are massively marking up their products.

In some cases, they are marking up their products by 20 TIMES more than they cost to make.

That £2,450 St. Laurent purse currently on sale on their website?  It actually only cost them £124.37 to make!  

While most people expect a business to make some profit on their products, this kind of insane market is akin to highway robbery.

It was after three years of working behind the scenes and seeing the TRUE cost of these purses that the assistant finally revealed the truth.

"I'm from a small town in the midwest," says the assistant, who has chosen to remain anonymous. "All my life my friends and I dreamed of being able to afford a Chanel or Louis Vuitton purse."

Working there was "a dream job" for the assistant, but as time went on, she became disillusioned.

"When I saw that even the most expensive purses on the market, the ones costing £3500 or more, were only costing the designers £150 in total costs... I couldn't believe it!" she continued.  "I felt like the entire industry was a lie.  Most businesses would be happy if they could double their profits.  But a 20 times markup? It made me sick!"

Frustrated and angry over feeling like these top brands were lying to their customers, she went public with the news.

"It's time for change," she said to me as we met up over tea in downtown Manhattan.  "I've gone to the manufacturers that actually make the purses for all the top designers and cut a deal with them.  They're sending us the EXACT purses you'll find for £2,000 or more, but without all the markup. That means my friends can finally enjoy a purse exactly like the Chanel one... for LESS than £100!"

As I left the cafe that day, I gripped my Hermes handbag a bit tighter.  While I loved it, was it really worth the £1,685 that I paid for it? If I could have the exact bag, minus the silver logo on the side, for £100, would I still want it?

You bet I would. Until now, we've just never been given the choice.

As I walked back to the office that day, there was a cold wind blowing.  Pulling my scarf up around my neck, I found the wind appropriate. There is indeed a cold wind blowing for the fashion industry.

Technology and supply chains have made entry into the luxury market much easier than in years past.  And if anyone can disrupt the Luxury Purse market, she can.
